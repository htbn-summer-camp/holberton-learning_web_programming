Food and restaurants

Mexican food in the Mission District

There's more to Mexican food than just taco and burritos. In the Mission district, you will find great and authentic mexican, peruvian, nicaraguan food. Our recommendation is Loló, a restaurant that embraces the artistic spirit of the Mission district with a fusion of Latin american cuisine and a unique decor that embraces the spirit of the Mission District.

Loló - 974 Valencia St, San Francisco, CA 94110

Japanese cuisine in Japantown

Japantown is a neighborhood comprised of just six square blocks, where you can things to do, see, and of course eat. There are a lot of restaurants in this small area that offer different kinds of japanese cuisine. One restaurant we recommend is Marufuku, where you can eat delicious and tasty ramen noodles. You might have to wait in line for some time, but it is worth the wait.

Marufuku Ramen - 1581 Webster St #235, San Francisco, CA 94115

Enjoy a meal in the calm neighborhood of outer Richmond

This area is less active than the more famous places in San Francisco like Chinatown or the Mission district, but it does have its load of great restaurants : italian, chinese, japanese... you name it. The PPQ Dungeness Island restaurant, featuring Dungeness crab specialties & traditional Vietnamese, is definitely worth a try. There, you can eat delicious crab, or more traditional vietnamese dishes.

PPQ Dungeness Island - 2332 Clement St, San Francisco, CA 94121

Discover the restaurants of Hayes Valley

In the cool and revitalized athmosphere of Hayes Valley, a neighborhood built around its main commercial street Hayes street, you will find shops, a community garden, and various restaurants that offer a culinary diversity. Mr Benjamin is a modern restaurant in Hayes Valley inspired by the parisian bistro culture and that offers a great experience of French cuisine.

Mr Benjamin - 451 Gough St, San Francisco, CA 94102
